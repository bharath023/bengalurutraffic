Every second of signal delay is linear and compounding — it multiplies across every signal, every trip, every working day. A 10s delay at 3 signals/km over a 10km commute means 5 minutes lost per trip → 41.7 hours per year → more than 5 full working days.

At Silk Board's 250s cycle with 65s green phase and 2.2s saturation headway (HCM 6th Ed):

Vehicle #29 → clears in 63.8 seconds → just makes green
Vehicle #30 → clears in 66 seconds → misses by 1 second → waits another 250 seconds

This is the cliff effect — a discontinuous jump caused entirely by cycle length. Reducing the cycle from 250s to 180s (IRC:93 compliance) would eliminate it entirely. No flyover, no grade separator, no ₹13,262 crore addresses this.

Evidence-backed interventions — what the data actually demands
Move fewer vehicles
smarter. Not more
vehicles faster.
🚦
Scale BATCS — AI signals
900+ junctions city-wide. Only 165 run AI (C-DAC CoSiCoSt). Scaling to 500 junctions → estimated 20–30% intersection delay reduction. Hudson Circle pilot: −33% travel time. ₹150 Cr already budgeted. Zero construction. Zero land acquisition.
Quick win · Zero construction
💰
Congestion Pricing (ERP)
CBD cordon pricing ₹50–150/crossing at peak hours. Singapore ERP (LTA, 1998–2005): −44% CBD vehicle entry, −33% travel delays. Stockholm (2006): −21% traffic. Projected Bengaluru revenue: ₹400–600 Cr/yr → fund BMTC EV fleet directly.
Demand suppression · Proven globally
🚇
Close the Metro Gap
Weekday–weekend Metro gap: 29.5%. E-bike docking at all 83 stations converts last-mile trips. 60%+ of Bengaluru vehicle trips are under 5 km (DULT). Closing the gap adds ~38,000 daily journeys without a single new station or tunnel.
Last-mile integration
📋
Stagger + Quota
IT corridor time-band staggering (Whitefield, EC, Sarjapur) — Tokyo's 7-band system cut peak demand 25–30%. RTO zone vehicle quota: once zone exceeds saturation density (vehicles/road-km²), 3× registration fee. Singapore COE model adapted for Indian context.
Policy mandate · No infrastructure
B-SMILE spends ₹13,262 Cr to move more vehicles faster. These four interventions together cost a fraction of that — and address the demand side that flyovers never can. Silk Board's Sunday cycle is 31% shorter. The geometry isn't the problem. The volume is.
