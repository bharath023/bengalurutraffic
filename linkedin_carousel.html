Policy Framework — India-Appropriate Interventions · Grounded in Existing Legal & Institutional StructuresPractitioner analysis
Not Singapore. Not Amsterdam. What actually works within Indian frameworks.
Four tiers. Enforce, Deploy,
Reform, Invest — in that order.
TIER 1 ENFORCE — Zero new spend · BTP already has authority · 0–6 months IRC:93 · IPC · MV Act
IRC:93 signal compliance: BTP can reprogram 30+ violating fixed-time controllers to ≤180s peak cycle. No tender. No budget. Silk Board Sunday shows −31% is achievable today.
Strict lane discipline + dedicated 2-wheeler lanes: CRRI data: indiscipline reduces effective capacity by 30–40%. Dedicated 2W lanes at top-30 congested junctions using road paint + Advanced Stop Lines (ASL) — 2W advance to front of queue at red. Channels the 71.3% 2W fleet, eliminates weaving, improves saturation discharge. London, Paris, Amsterdam precedent. ATMS cameras enforce violations. Cost: road marking only.
Junction encroachment clearance: BBMP removes hawkers and illegal parking at top-30 signals. Recovers 20–40% lost capacity. BBMP has existing enforcement powers under BBMP Act 2020.
Night cycle reduction + heavy vehicle windows: Reprogram FIXED-mode junctions with 3 time-band profiles. Trucks restricted to arterials 10pm–6am (Mumbai model, 2007). MV Act executive notification — no new legislation.
TIER 2 DEPLOY — Already funded · Already designed · Just execute · 6–18 months ₹150Cr approved · BATCS · BMTC
BATCS scale-up: ₹150 Cr already approved for 354 junctions. C-DAC CoSiCoSt AI system. Hudson Circle: −33% travel time. Scale from 165 → 500 junctions. Indian technology, Indian funding, Indian execution.
Dedicated bus lanes on key arterials — strict enforcement: Hosur Road, Tumkur Road, Bellary Road all have 6+ lanes — dedicate one per direction to buses. Camera-enforced (ATMS stack). A single bus lane moves 4,000–8,000 passengers/hr vs 600–800 in a mixed car lane. Ahmedabad Janmarg BRTS reference (not Delhi BRT — that failed due to implementation, not concept). BMTC carries 30–35L pax/day on 0.7% of fleet — they deserve priority infrastructure.
BMTC EV fleet deployment: FAME-III + Karnataka EV Policy 2021 funds 300+ e-buses ordered. Deploy on trunk routes. Bus lanes without EV upgrade is incomplete — both together maximise impact.
Metro feeder para-transit integration: Pre-paid auto/2W taxi feeder bays at all 83 metro stations. Kochi Metro model (operational 2017). Formalise what Ola/Uber do informally at Majestic and Indiranagar stations today.
TIER 3 REFORM — Policy notifications within current frameworks · 6–24 months Karnataka Govt · BBMP · BMRDA
IT corridor staggered hours: Karnataka IT/BT Dept notification mandating 3 shift bands (8am / 10am / 12pm) for SEZ/IT park operating licences. Whitefield, EC, Sarjapur. Tokyo 7-band precedent. Legally feasible as SEZ operating condition — no new legislation.
ONDC for mobility: Extend Open Network Digital Commerce to transport aggregation — Namma Metro + BMTC + Namma Yatri auto + bike taxi under single QR/UPI flow. Ministry of Housing already piloting in 3 cities. Indian framework, Indian infra, global impact.
TIER 4 INVEST — Medium-term · Requires planning · 2–5 years BMRDA · NHAI · Smart Cities
BRT on Hosur Road + Tumkur Road: Ahmedabad BRTS model (not the failed Bengaluru 2009 BRT — that failed due to implementation, not concept). Dedicated bus lane with signal priority. Ahmedabad BRTS carries 1.3L passengers/day on ₹800Cr investment vs ₹13,262Cr flyovers.
TOD policy enforcement: BMRDA TOD notification 2021 already exists — grants 2 FSI bonus within 500m of metro stations. Enforce it. Reduces CBD-bound trip generation at source. No new law. Just implement the existing notification.
